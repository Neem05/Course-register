Course Detail

Course Name: Single Variable Calculus

Code: 19MAT101

Program: B.Tech. in Electrical and Electronics Engineering, B.Tech. in Civil Engineering, B.Tech. in Aerospace Engineering, B.Tech. in Chemical Engineering, B.Tech. in Mechanical Engineering, B.Tech. in Computer Science and Engineering, B.Tech. in Computer and Communication Engineering, B.Tech. in Electronics and Communication Engineering, B.Tech. in Electronics and Computer Engineering

Semester: One

Syllabus

Graphs

Functions and their Graphs. Shifting and Scaling of Graphs.

Limit and Continuity

Limit (One Sided and Two Sided) of Functions. Continuous Functions, Discontinuities, Monotonic Functions, Infinite Limits and Limit at Infinity.

Graphing

Extreme Values of Functions, Concavity and Curve Sketching.

Integration

Definite Integrals, The Mean Value Theorem for definite integrals, Fundamental Theorem of Calculus, Integration Techniques.

Evaluation Pattern

Test-1 - 25 marks (one hour test) after 8th lecture. CA – 25 marks (Quizzes / assignments / lab practice) Test – 2- 50 marks (two-hour test) at the end of 15th lecture. Total – 100 marks. Supplementary exam for this course will be conducted as a two-hour test for 100 marks.

Resources

  • 'Calculus' by G.B. Thomas Pearson Education, 2009, Eleventh Edition.
  • 'Calculus' by Monty J. Strauss, Gerald J. Bradley

Objectives and Outcomes

CO1 To understand the concepts of single variable calculus.
CO1 To understand and summarize technical documents.
CO2 To sketch graphs for functions using the concepts of single variable calculus and apply the fundamental theorem of calculus to evaluate integrals.
